# Chemistry Foundations

A structured learning path covering fundamental chemistry concepts essential for computational chemistry, materials science, and nanotechnology.

## Learning Path

### 1. Atomic Structure & Quantum Chemistry
- **Atomic models**: Bohr model, quantum mechanical model
- **Quantum numbers**: n, l, m_l, m_s
- **Electron configurations**: Aufbau principle, Hund's rule, Pauli exclusion
- **Orbital shapes**: s, p, d, f orbitals
- **Atomic spectra**: emission, absorption, energy transitions
- **Periodic trends**: electronegativity, ionization energy, atomic radius

**Applications**: Electronic structure calculations, spectroscopy

### 2. Chemical Bonding
- **Ionic bonding**: electron transfer, lattice energy, Born-Haber cycle
- **Covalent bonding**: Lewis structures, VSEPR theory, molecular geometry
- **Metallic bonding**: electron sea model, band theory
- **Intermolecular forces**: 
  - Van der Waals (London dispersion, dipole-dipole)
  - Hydrogen bonding
  - Ion-dipole interactions
- **Molecular orbital theory**: LCAO, bonding/antibonding orbitals
- **Hybridization**: sp, sp², sp³, dsp³, d²sp³

**Applications**: Material properties, molecular modeling, drug design

### 3. Thermodynamics & Thermochemistry
- **Laws of thermodynamics**: 0th, 1st, 2nd, 3rd laws
- **State functions**: internal energy (U), enthalpy (H), entropy (S), Gibbs free energy (G)
- **Enthalpy changes**: ΔH_f (formation), ΔH_c (combustion), ΔH_rxn (reaction)
- **Hess's law**: energy path independence
- **Entropy & spontaneity**: ΔS, ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
- **Chemical equilibrium**: K_eq, Le Chatelier's principle
- **Phase transitions**: melting, boiling, sublimation

**Applications**: Reaction prediction, energy storage, phase diagrams

### 4. Chemical Kinetics
- **Reaction rates**: rate laws, rate constants
- **Order of reactions**: zero, first, second order
- **Arrhenius equation**: k = A·e^(-E_a/RT)
- **Activation energy**: transition state theory
- **Catalysis**: homogeneous, heterogeneous, enzyme catalysis
- **Reaction mechanisms**: elementary steps, rate-determining step
- **Collision theory**: molecular dynamics

**Applications**: Catalysis design, reaction optimization, drug metabolism

### 5. Electrochemistry
- **Oxidation-reduction**: redox reactions, oxidation states
- **Electrochemical cells**: galvanic, electrolytic
- **Standard electrode potentials**: E° values, Nernst equation
- **Batteries**: primary, secondary, fuel cells
- **Corrosion**: mechanisms, prevention
- **Electrolysis**: Faraday's laws

**Applications**: Energy storage, sensors, electrodeposition

### 6. Solutions & Colloidal Chemistry
- **Concentration units**: molarity, molality, mole fraction, ppm
- **Solubility**: like dissolves like, solubility rules
- **Colligative properties**: vapor pressure lowering, boiling point elevation, freezing point depression, osmotic pressure
- **Colloids**: types, Tyndall effect, Brownian motion
- **Surface phenomena**: surface tension, adsorption, surfactants

**Applications**: Drug formulation, nanoparticle synthesis, separation techniques

### 7. Acid-Base Chemistry
- **Theories**: Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, Lewis
- **pH scale**: pH = -log[H⁺], pOH, pKa, pKb
- **Buffer solutions**: Henderson-Hasselbalch equation
- **Titrations**: strong-strong, weak-strong, polyprotic acids
- **Hydrolysis**: salt solutions

**Applications**: Biochemistry, environmental chemistry, analytical chemistry

### 8. Organic Chemistry Basics
- **Functional groups**: alcohols, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, amines, ethers, esters
- **Nomenclature**: IUPAC naming conventions
- **Isomerism**: structural, geometric (cis-trans), optical (enantiomers)
- **Organic reactions**: 
  - Substitution (SN1, SN2)
  - Elimination (E1, E2)
  - Addition reactions
  - Condensation reactions
- **Polymers**: addition, condensation, biological polymers

**Applications**: Drug design, materials science, biochemistry

### 9. Spectroscopy & Analytical Methods
- **UV-Vis spectroscopy**: Beer-Lambert law, chromophores
- **IR spectroscopy**: vibrational modes, fingerprint region
- **NMR spectroscopy**: chemical shift, spin-spin coupling
- **Mass spectrometry**: molecular weight, fragmentation patterns
- **Chromatography**: GC, HPLC, principles of separation

**Applications**: Molecular characterization, quality control, structural determination

### 10. Computational Chemistry Introduction
- **Molecular mechanics**: force fields, energy minimization
- **Quantum chemistry methods**: Hartree-Fock, DFT, post-HF methods
- **Basis sets**: STO-3G, 6-31G, cc-pVDZ
- **Molecular dynamics**: integration algorithms, ensembles
- **Visualization**: molecular structures, orbitals, surfaces

**Applications**: Drug discovery, materials design, reaction prediction

## Prerequisites
- Basic mathematics (algebra, trigonometry)
- Understanding of scientific notation
- Basic physics concepts (energy, forces)

## Recommended Resources
- Textbooks: Atkins' Physical Chemistry, McMurry's Organic Chemistry
- Software: Avogadro, PyMOL, Gaussian, ORCA
- Python libraries: RDKit, ASE (Atomic Simulation Environment), PySCF
